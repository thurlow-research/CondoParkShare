# CondoParkShare — Information for HOA Boards & Property Managers

*A shared parking amenity for your building. This sheet explains what it is, what it costs, and what's asked of you.*

---

## What it is

CondoParkShare lets your residents share parking spots. When an owner is away, they list their spot; another resident can book it by the hour for the time they need. It turns idle parking into a convenience for the whole building — no new construction, no attendant, no spreadsheets.

Community chat stays wherever it already lives (Discord, email, your portal). CondoParkShare just handles the listing and booking.

## How residents use it

- **List:** "I'm away these dates" — a couple of taps, and the spot is offered.
- **Book:** search the time you need, pick an available spot, done.
- **Earn priority:** residents who list their spots regularly earn the ability to book further in advance — a built-in nudge that keeps spots flowing into the pool. There's no points-for-cash scheme; it's simply a fairness mechanism that rewards sharing.

## What it costs — three options, your choice

You decide the model when the building is set up:

1. **Free forever.** No charge to anyone. (This is how the pilot building runs.)
2. **Building-funded.** The HOA or manager pays a low per-unit subscription — about **$1 per unit per month** (the low end of the HOA-software market). Residents pay nothing and exchange freely. Only the building provides a payment method.
3. **Resident-funded.** If the building prefers not to carry the cost, residents who want the service pay a small flat monthly fee themselves. The building pays nothing.

**Every option starts with a free promotional period (typically 90 days)** so residents can try it and a healthy pool of listings can form before any billing begins. You're notified well before any charge starts, and you can stop at any time during the trial at no cost.

## What's asked of the building

- **Authorize the service** for your property (and confirm your governing documents permit it if a fee will apply).
- **Choose the payer model** above.
- **Help residents join** by distributing invite links (residents can't sign up off the open internet — it's gated to your building).
- That's it. There's no hardware, no integration, and no day-to-day administration required from the board; spot and resident management is handled through a simple admin view.

## Privacy & data

- Resident accounts are protected with two-factor authentication.
- Personal information is encrypted, access is limited and logged, and residents can have their data deleted on request.
- Each building's data is fully isolated from every other building's.
- The service is built to be privacy-regulation-aware (GDPR-style protections).

## Why it's low-risk to try

- Starts free; no commitment during the promo period.
- No fixed expense unless you choose the building-funded option — and even then it's a small, predictable per-unit figure you can stop.
- Nothing to install or maintain.
- If it doesn't catch on in your building, you simply let it lapse.

## In one line

*A low-cost, low-effort amenity that turns your residents' empty parking spots into a shared convenience — free to try, with a payment model you choose only if and when it proves its value.*
