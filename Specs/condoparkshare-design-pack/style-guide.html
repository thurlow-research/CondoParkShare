CondoParkShare Shared parking for your building

Brand & UI Style Guide

A calm, trustworthy way for neighbors to share an open spot.

CondoParkShare turns an empty deeded parking space into a convenience for the whole building. The identity is quiet and precise like good signage, warmed by an eco-green palette — utility first, community close behind. Its one working idea: green means available, clay means booked, so color does real work everywhere it appears.

01 — Color

Greens that read as trust and growth, grounded in warm concrete neutrals.

Two greens carry meaning, not decoration: Meadow signals an available spot and primary actions; Clay (the one warm counterpoint) marks a booked spot. Everything else stays neutral so those signals never compete.

06 — Voice & tone

Plain, active, neighborly. Name things by what people do.

Calm and clear, never salesy or cute. An action keeps its name through the whole flow — the button that says “Book this spot” leads to a confirmation that says “Booked.”

✓ Do
“List your spot while you're away.” · “Book this spot.” · “Your time's almost up — heading back?” · “No spots open then. Try a wider window.”
✕ Don't
“Submit availability.” · “Utilize the booking module.” · “Oops! Something went wrong.” · “Monetize your parking asset.”

Principles: active voice; one consistent name per action; errors explain what to do next, no apologizing; empty states invite action (“List the first spot in your building”); sentence case throughout.
